ANALYST COMMENTARY
Priya Mehta · Post-season breakdown
The thesis going in was straightforward: retail participants tend to over-index on familiar names — the large ITcos, the banks they already bank with, the auto brands they see on roads. That's not a bad instinct, but it's a lagging one. By the time a stock is in everyone's portfolio, the easy returns are priced in.

I went against the grain on consumption. TITAN and DMART were underweighted by most participants. The premium consumption recovery story had strong fundamentals — Q3 earnings beat, margin recovery, and a tailwind that fundamental research surfaces before prices react.

The three most popular retail picks — INFY, TCS, TATAMOTORS — all underperformed. It's not that these are bad companies. It's about entry timing and macro context. IT faced deal pipeline pressure; auto faced input cost margins. Participants who swapped into pharma or consumption at the Day 30 rebalance narrowed the gap significantly. That's the argument for active research — knowing when sector rotation is coming, not when it's already happened.
